About Me

I am currently a senior at Hunter College, majoring in Computer Science with a minor in Mathematics, and I’m expected to graduate in Spring 2026. My concentration is in Data Science and Data Engineering, and I have hands-on experience building machine learning models and creating compelling data visualizations. Beyond my work in tech, I am extremely creative. I love drawing and digital art, enjoy making jewelry, and have a deep passion for fashion and especially runway fashion. My love for design inspired me to take this digital media course to refine my creative skills and explore new ways to bring my artistic ideas to life.

Creative Statement

My creative work focuses on using digital tools to explore design and self-expression. I am interested in how color, visuals, and composition can be used to communicate ideas and emotions. Fashion and digital art influence my creative process, especially the creativity and experimentation seen in runway fashion. Through this digital media course, I hope to improve my design skills and try new creative techniques while creating visually engaging work.

Week 3: Shallow Depth Of Field

The original image shows a shallow depth of field taken with a Canon camera using an aperture of f/4.5, ISO 800, an exposure time of 1/500 second, and a focal length of 51mm. It was harder than I expected to achieve a strong shallow depth of field using the camera settings alone so it took alot of trial and error to get the settings correct. The original photo came out darker than I intended, so I adjusted the exposure in the Camera Raw filter to brighten it and bring out more detail. I also increased the clarity, reduced noise, and clipped around the coffee cup to help it stand out more clearly from the background. To emphasize the shallow depth of field effect, I applied a lens blur to the background so the focus would be on the Dunkin cup. Even though the a Dunkin cup is pretty simple, this project helped me better understand how camera settings and editing can be used to create depth. I’m still getting comfortable using manual settings, but I hope to keep improving so I can apply these techniques to more interesting subjects in the future.

Week 3: Deep Depth Of Field

These photos demonstrate a deep depth of field because everything in the frame is clearly visible, including the grass, building, trees, and sky. I took the image using a Kodak EasyShare camera. The original photo was slightly underexposed and not framed as well as it could have been, so I adjusted the exposure and highlights in the Camera Raw filter to brighten the image and bring out more detail. I also used the auto-crop feature to improve the overall composition and center the photo better. I really enjoyed editing this photo because I was able to notice small details that I hadn’t fully appreciated when I first took it.The photo looks alot clearer and easier to focus on. This is my favorite piece I’ve created for this class so far because of its serene and calm atmosphere, which I think comes through more after the editing.

Week 3: Photoshop Shallow Depth Of Field

I created a shallow depth of field effect for this image using Photoshop. The photo was originally taken with a Kodak EasyShare camera, and at first it looked pretty flat because both the subject and the background were equally clear. I clipped the puppy and added a layer mask, then applied a lens blur to the background to create more separation and emulate a shallow depth of field. Even after blurring the background, the puppy still blended in, so I increased the contrast, added texture, and increased the clarity to make the subject stand out more. Clipping the dog was challenging because of the fur, which made it difficult to get a perfectly clean selection. The puppy looks slightly like a sticker against the background. However, this project helped me understand how clipping and selections work in Photoshop, and I hope to improve my clipping skills going forward.
