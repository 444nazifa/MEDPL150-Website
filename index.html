About Me

I am currently a senior at Hunter College, majoring in Computer Science with a minor in Mathematics, and I’m expected to graduate in Spring 2026. My concentration is in Data Science and Data Engineering, and I have hands-on experience building machine learning models and creating compelling data visualizations. Beyond my work in tech, I am extremely creative. I love drawing and digital art, enjoy making jewelry, and have a deep passion for fashion and especially runway fashion. My love for design inspired me to take this digital media course to refine my creative skills and explore new ways to bring my artistic ideas to life.

Creative Statement

My creative work focuses on using digital tools to explore design and self-expression. I am interested in how color, visuals, and composition can be used to communicate ideas and emotions. Fashion and digital art influence my creative process, especially the creativity and experimentation seen in runway fashion. Through this digital media course, I hope to improve my design skills and try new creative techniques while creating visually engaging work.

Week 2: Thomas Hunter

I chose this photo because I thought the ceiling lights looked visually interesting and created a strong sense of repetition. The lights naturally draw the viewer’s eye down the hallway and give the image a clear sense of depth. When I first took the photo, it was slightly blurry, so I refined it by adjusting the focus and sharpening the image to make the details clearer. Cleaning up the image helped emphasize the structure of the hallway and made the lighting stand out more, which improved the overall composition.

View of Hunter College's third-floor sky bridge.
5"x7"

Week 2: Sky Bridge

I found this photo interesting because I liked the overall scene and the way the different elements came together, especially the buildings and street. I focused on color and contrast to make the photo feel more dynamic. I edited the image to make the colors more vibrant and to increase contrast, which helped different elements in the scene stand out from each other. The stronger colors make the image feel more engaging, while the contrast helps guide the viewer’s attention through the photo. These adjustments helped the photo look less bland and made the composition more visually impactful.
